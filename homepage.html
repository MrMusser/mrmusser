About Me

I am a recent graduate of Georgetown University, where I studied government with a concentration in political theory. My primary area of interest is in the role that technology plays in transforming political systems. To even broach this topic requires a wide array of competencies; anyone studying politics and technology must know a bit about the science underlying new and emerging technologies, the sociology of political systems, the policy development process, and the philosophical presuppositions of political systems. For the past several years, I've been attempting to build up as many of these skills as possible. The content on this page should demonstrate my skills in the areas of policy research, computer science (primarily shown through a portfolio of some data visualization projects), and general writing ability.

Some of my upcoming projects include: a paper using natural language processing to examine the differences in the way that scientists, bioethicists, and government commissions speak to the public about CRISPR; an ethics paper discussing the political ethics of investment in new technologies; and a short story exploring the potential social impact of widespread pet cloning. Check back in soon to see more of my work!
